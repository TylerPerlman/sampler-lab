# Changelog

All notable public changes are recorded here.

## 0.12.0 - Feature-complete beta

- Added the replicated capability-aware benchmark across IID, weighted, particle, Markov,
  adaptive, gradient, Hessian, ensemble, variational, Stein, and learned samplers.
- Added common Gaussian-mixture, anisotropic-funnel, and bimodal-funnel reports with exact
  references, explicit exclusions, separate training/evaluation costs, JSON, CSV, Markdown, and
  optional figures.
- Added public API, benchmarking, and capability, provenance, and reference documentation.
- Stabilized output-semantics and exactness labels for benchmark results.
- Added `sampler-lab-benchmark`.

## 0.11.0

- Added rare-event and small-noise importance-sampling methods, exact Gaussian tail oracles,
  Laplace approximations, twisting, tempering, and multiple-dominating-point mixtures.

## 0.10.0

- Added adaptive MCMC, policy-gradient proposal learning, variational proposals, KSD, SVGD, and
  exact frozen-evaluation protocols.

## 0.9.0

- Added affine conditioning, Hessian repair, stochastic Newton, stretch/walk ensembles, and
  Rosenbrock benchmarks.

## 0.8.0

- Added Hamiltonian, HMC, underdamped, involutive-correction, and XY-model methods.

## 0.7.0

- Added generator limits, Euler--Maruyama, ULA, preconditioning, MALA, and Gaussian bias analysis.

## 0.6.0

- Added annealed paths, Jarzynski/AIS estimators, annealed SMC, and free-energy diagnostics.

## 0.5.0

- Added generic Metropolis--Hastings, Gibbs schedules, and the exact small-lattice Ising laboratory.

## 0.4.0

- Added finite-state Markov operators, invariant laws, reversibility, Poisson equations, spectral
  diagnostics, and exact asymptotic variance.

## 0.3.0

- Added particle clouds, resampling, SIS, ancestry, and self-avoiding-walk counting.

## 0.2.0

- Added importance sampling, self-normalization, weight diagnostics, and Gaussian-tail studies.

## 0.1.0

- Added the typed project foundation, IID estimators, exact transforms, rejection sampling, and
  unit-disk experiments.
